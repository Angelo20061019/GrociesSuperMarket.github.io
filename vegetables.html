OUR VENUS

LOCAL FRAMS

Local farms supply fresh, seasonal vegetables to nearby supermarkets. These farms often have direct partnerships with the stores, ensuring quick delivery and supporting the local economy. This approach provides customers with high-quality produce while reducing transportation time and costs.

WHOLESALE PRODUCE MARKETS

Wholesale produce markets aggregate vegetables from various growers, offering a wide range of options in large quantities. Supermarkets source from these markets to meet their diverse and consistent supply needs. Examples include large markets like the Hunts Point Produce Market in New York City.

AGRICULTURAL COOPERATIVES & DISTRIBUTION CENTER

Agricultural cooperatives are groups of farmers who combine their resources to market and distribute their produce more effectively. Distribution centers handle the logistics of storing and transporting vegetables from farms to supermarkets, ensuring fresh and efficiently delivered produce. Major grocery chains often operate their own distribution networks to streamline this process.
